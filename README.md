**The complex-market offer engine for Claude Code.** Paste a complicated market — an AI-written industry article, a vendor call transcript, a dense thesis — and Offer-Builder simplifies it, finds the gap, and constructs a no-brainer offer you can hand straight to your cold-email copywriter. It builds the offer. It never writes the email.

---

## What This Is

Most people walking into a sophisticated B2B market drown in the language. They confuse the *mechanism* (GTM engineering, outbound, AI implementation) with the *offer*. Offer-Builder fixes that. It does the two jobs that have to happen **before** a single word of copy gets written: it makes you actually understand the market — the problem, who owns it, how it shifts by company size — and then it constructs the offer, anchored to a gap and priced as loss avoidance, so anybody who sees it would feel stupid saying no. The output is built to survive a cold email, then handed to the copywriter.

        │

  PHASE B — construct the no-brainer offer

## How It Builds

Offer-Builder runs on one belief: **people move for urgency, not desire.** A nicer future never beats stopping an expensive, ongoing loss. So every offer is anchored to a gap and priced *next to that gap* — buying reads as loss avoided, not money spent. It is a problem-solver, never a service provider. The mechanism stays under the hood; the offer is always the end result the prospect gets without the painful thing. And because a no-brainer in a sales letter dies in a cold email, every offer is built to survive compression to one outcome, one soft ask, in believable, in-motion language.

---

## Tips for Users

- **Feed it the messy version.** The denser and more jargon-heavy the source, the more value it adds — that's the whole point.
- **Be honest about your active position.** It will ask what's truthfully in motion. Don't inflate it; the copywriter needs a real anchor.
- **Build several offers, then run the matrix.** Ask "are these the same offer?" — it'll map one offer across many industries.
- **It won't write your email.** That's by design. Take the Offer Sheet to `/proximity`.
